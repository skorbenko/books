Atomic Habits by James Clear

  • To write a good book, one has to become a good book.

  • A crazy story about how James rebuilt his life after a baseball accident:

    • He was a senior in high school and was on the baseball team when a bat flew out of the batter’s hands and into James’ face.

    • The bat broke his nose, eye sockets.

    • James was rushed to the hospital, almost died.

    • Applied habits to get back on track, and once that worked, used them to go above and beyond.

  • Habits have this powerful effect, therefore they build out systems as opposed to goals -- more useful in the long run.

  • Goals are the results one wants to achieve, and systems are the processes that lead to said results.

  • Winners and losers have the same goal - to win. We only interview the winners, so there is a confirmation bias.

  • Goals restrict one’s happiness.

  • Change the underlying beliefs that led to your past behavior.

  • “Identity” derived from the Latin “essentitas”- being, and “identidem”, which means repeatedly.

  • You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems.

  • Be 1% “better” every day.

    • (1+0.01)^365=37.7834343329

    • (1-0.01)^365=0.02551796445

  • When the habits are set up and healthy, one is free to work on the other aspects of one’s life.

  • Identity based habits > Outcome based habits

    • You do not want to read a book, you want to become a voracious reader.

    • You do not want to journal every once in a while, you want to be the person who journals every day.

    • The identity is proven to oneself if one does the activity every day - works on the habit.

  • How to Create a Good Habit:

    • 1 - Cue: Make it obvious

      • Set your clothes out the night before so that it is easier to go out for a run than to change into formalwear.

      • “Until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life and you will call it fate.” ― C.G. Jung

      • One does not have to be the victim of one’s enviroment, but can be the architect.

      • Society has created cues (dopamine-fueled feedback loops) that are much more powerful than anything our ancestors had.

    • 2 - Craving: Make it attractive

    • 3 - Response: Make it easy

    • 4 - Reward: Make it satisfying

      • As opposed to B. F. Skinner’s

  • Cue => routine => reward

  • How to Break a Bad Habit:

    • 1 - Cue: Make it invisible

    • 2 - Craving: Make it unattractive

    • 3 - Response: Make it difficult

    • 4 - Reward: Make it unsatisfying

  • If a behavior is insufficient in any of the four stages, it will not become a habit.

  • Eliminate the cue and your habit will never start.

  • Reduce the craving and you won’t experience enough motivation to act.

  • Make the behavior difficult and you won’t be able to do it.

  • And if the reward fails to satisfy your desire, then you’ll have no reason to do it again in the future.

  • Without the first three steps, a behavior will not occur.

  • Without all four, the behavior will not be repeated.

  • Habit stacking - creating a domino effect where the reward of each habit is another habit, so the first habit acts as a cue.

  • Does this behavior help me become the type of person I wish to be?

  • Implementation intention: “When situation X arises, I will perform response Y.”

  • People with a specific plan for when and where to perform a habit are much more likely to follow through

    • When the predetermined moment occurs, there is no need to think, one just follows through.

  • Most people think they lack motivation, but they really just lack clarity.

  • People we imitate

    • The close

      • There is a 57% greater chance of becoming obese if one of your friends is obese.

    • The many

      • When in doubt, humans look to the group.

    • The powerful

    • This is why mastermind groups are so powerful.

  • Take a break from the space where you do your daily work, which is also linked to your current thought patterns.

  • Create a separate space for work, study, exercise

    • One space, one use.

    • So that each context is associated with each enviroment.

    • Therefore sleep will be easier in the bedroom when that is the only action that is done there.

  • Walk slowly, but never backwards.

  • 90% of heroin addicts relapse once they return home to the same neighborhood with all the same cues.

  • The fact that you are learning means nothing if you do nothing with it.

  • Daydreaming about the vacation is often more enjoyable than being on vacation.

    • Join a culture where your desired behavior is the normal behavior.

    • Surround yourself with people who have the habits you want to have.

  • Life is predictive because every action is predicted before it is completed.

  • To master a habit, start with repetition, not perfection.

  • Look for every point of friction and eliminate it.

  • A good day vs. a bad day often comes down to a few productive achoices at decisive moments.

    • The choices stack up throughout the days, weeks, months, quarters, years.

  • A new habit should take less than two minutes to do.

  • The habit of showing up is fundamental.

  • What is immediately rewarded is repeated. What is immediately punished is avoided.

  • The costs of your good habits are in the present. The costs of your bad habits are in the future.

  • Visual measures provide clear evidence of progress.

  • One must evade conflicting identities i.e. rewarding a gym session with a bowl of ice cream.

  • The act of tracking one’s behavior can create the urge to change it.

  • Habit tracking keeps one honest.

  • Never fail more than once.

  • One’s goal starts the habit.

  • It’s crucial to show up when you don’t feel like it.

  • Understand your nature to better your strategy.

  • Genes tell you where the odds are in your favor.

  • Tasks that are 4% tougher than one’s ability is much more fulfilling than tougher or easier tasks.

  • Keep a decision journal.

    • Topics to include:

      • The decisions you make

      • Why you make them

      • The conditions for the decision

      • The context

      • What the expected outcome is

    • Review it every week or every month.

  • Professionals know what is important to them and work towards the goal with a purpose.

  • One’s identity creates a kind of “pride” that encourages one to deny one’s weak spots and prevents one from truly growing.

  • Happiness is the absence of desire.

  • Craving is the desire to fix anything and everything.
