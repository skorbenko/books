The One Thing by Gary Keller

  • Success leaves clues -- most successful companies are known for one thing only.
  • Extremely Pareto's principle:
    • (80%)^3 : (20%)^3 => 51.2% : 0.8%
  • The 6 Lies
    • Everything Matters Equally -- some tasks have a larger impact.
    • Multitasking -- real multitasking does not exist. When one tries to "multitask" one is just continually switching between tasks, losing time on the transitions.
    • A Disciplined Life -- Life is not a marathon, it is a series of sprints. Success is a sprint just long enough to establish habits - from 18 to 254 days. No one is disciplined, people just train habits into their life. Some discipline is, however, required.
    • Willpower is always available on request -- willpower is able to be replenished via rest. What are my default answers? Introduce friction to stop default answers.
    • A Balanced Life -- does not exist. Sometimes one focuses on one area of life, sometime on the other, and then the leftover areas suffer a bit.
    • Big is Bad -- One must be open to the possibility that through the right things, one's life can become great.
  • What is the one thing I can do such that by doing it, everything else can be easier or unnecessary? -- it is important to ask this question on a regular basis.
    • "Judge a man by his questions rather than his answers" - Voltaire
    • The goal should be big and specific.
    • Extraordinary results require a great answer and require one to live outside one's comfort zone -- go big or go home.
    • The goal must be so far out there so that if things continue as they are, there would be no way to achieve said goal.
  • The 3 Commitments
    • Follow the path of mastery
      • Mastery is a path, not a destination
      • Mastery is giving the best one can to become the best one can be at one's most important work
    • Move from E to P
      • Move from the entrepreneurial (natural) way of doing things to the purposeful.
      • Use systems and focus to drive up willpower to achieve grand things.
    • Live the accountability cycle
      • Do not settle for the victim mentality
      • One can either be the author of their life, or the victim of it
      • Figure out what you can control and manipulate that
  • The 4 Thieves
    • Inability to say no
    • Fear of chaos
    • Poor health habits
    • Environment does not support one's goals
  • Purpose, Priority, Productivity
    • What is your "why"? Without a "why", the destination of one's work/productivity is burnout.
    • Priority should only be used in the singular
    • Someday goal => five year goal => yearly goal => monthly goal => weekly goal => the one thing one can do today
    • Do not fall for hyperbolic discounting
    • Use timeblocking to commit time in advance
      • Schedule time off first
      • Maker vs. manager time
      • An hour per week to review one's goals
    • Money is a metaphor for producing results
      • One's results come from the system one is using
      • Assign a dollar value to the system you are using
