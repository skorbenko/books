The Happiness Advantage by Shawn Achor

  • Happy people are productive people.
  • The Seven Principles
    1. The Happiness Advantage
      • Happiness is pleasure combined with deeper feelings of meaning and purpose.
      • Happiness is human flourishing.
    2. The Fulcrum & the Lever
      • Happiness is not about lying to ourselves; it is about adjusting our brains so that we can see above our circumstances.
      • Our external reality is far more dependent on the eyes of the viewer then we think
      • The Workaholic’s Curse: viewing family time and relaxation as unproductive, which makes said time unproductive instead of relaxing.
      • View your work as a calling, not a job or a career.
    3. The Tetris Effect
      • Paradise becomes hell when all you can see are the problems.
      • Lawyers are 3.6x likely to suffer from depression than the general population. They are taught to be critical, not accepting.
      • Can be stopped/improved via
        • Happiness
        • Gratitude
        • Optimism
    4. Falling Up
      • Our mind creates a map with three paths in the face of adversity:
        1. The situation stays as it is, circling around.
        2. The situation turns negative.
        3. One becomes stronger than ever before.
      • The ability to find the optimal path depends largely on how one views the circumstances - whether one is happy or not.
    5. The Zorro Circle
      • One shall limit one’s focus to a small circle before one can expand.
      • One could set a time limit and only work on current tasks as an example of a circle.
      • There are two modes for our brain - the jerk and the thinker. (Quick and complex/rational thinking)
      • Make two lists: things you can control, and things you can’t.
    6. The 20-Second Rule
      • Humans are mere bundles of habits
      • Willpower is not the answer
      • Common sense =/= common action
      • If there’s something you want to do, lower the required activation energy. The same principle can be inverted.
      • How can one change one’s defaults?
    7. Social Investment
      • Relationships are vital to health; they are hardwired into our biology.
      • For one, being a reliable friend is important before one needs a friend.
      • One can always invest in an offensive line.
      • Glue guys - people who hold teams together, but one can’t tell what exactly they do.
      • The response you have to other people is incredibly important.
      • A passive response to good news is a negative response.
      • Gratitude sparks upward growth.
  • The Broadening Effect: Positive emotions flood one’s brain with dopamine and serotonin, they improve one’s learning centers (easier to memorize and learn), make one keep information longer & retreat it faster, allows one to think quicker and more creatively, more skilled with complex problem solving.
  • One’s happiness levels can be increased using:
    • Conscious acts of kindness
    • Finding something to look forward to
    • Spending money on experiences
    • Exercise
    • Meditation
    • You can choose to be happy, and the circumstances will change.
