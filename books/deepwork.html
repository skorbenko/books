Deep Work by Cal Newport

    • Definitions
      • Deep work  — tasks that create new value and are hard to replicate, tasks that push cognitive capabilities to their limit, tasks that are performed free from distractions.
      • Shallow work - deep work’s opposite.
    • Successful types in the new economy:
      1. High-skilled workers (data visualisation, analytics, rapid prototyping)
      2. Superstars (remote work, design, consulting)
      3. Owners (VCs, access to capital)
    • Needed to thrive:
      1. Quickly master hard things
      2. Speed and quality of production
    • Deep work is:
      1. Valuable
      2. Rare
      3. Meaningful

    • Take breaks from focus, not from distractions
      • Schedule blocks where the internet is allowed, not when it isn't*

    • Meditate productively
      • Take a period of physical but not mental occupation (walking)
      • Use it to work on a well-defined mental task (working on a proof)
      • Bring your attention back when it wanders/stalls
      • Improves distraction resistance and sharpens concentration
