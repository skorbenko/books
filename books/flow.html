Flow by Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi

  • Flow is optimal experience.
  • Optimal experience occurs when a person’s body or mind is stretched to its limit in a voluntary effort to accomplish something difficult or worthwhile.
  • The goal is not a comfortable life; it is a life worth living.
  • The more one is in flow, the happier one is.
  • 8 Components of Enjoyment
    1. Confront tasks one can complete
    2. One concentrates
    3. The task has clear goals
    4. The task provides immediate feedback
    5. Removes awareness of everyday life
    6. Allows to exercise a sense of control
    7. The sense of yourself disappears, but is even stronger afterwards
    8. The sense of the duration of time seems altered
  • Happiness is not something that happens; it is a condition that must be prepared for, cultivated and defended privately by each person.
  • The more one gets into Flow, the easier it becomes.
  • One must read books that are above one’s level, looking up words in the dictionary.
  • Ways to experience Flow at work:
    1. Change the constraints and opportunities for expressing freedom/creativity
    2. Change the job itself until the conditions are conducive to Flow
  • 50% of people experience apathy during leisure
  • For most people, 50% of the time at work is spent in Flow
  • A major catastrophe that frustrates a central goal of life will either destroy the self, forcing a person to use all his psychic energy to erect a barrier around remaining goals, or it will provide a new, more clear and more urgent goal to overcome the challenges of the defeat.
  • It is not healthy when one is locked into a sense of Flow in only one sphere of his or her life.
